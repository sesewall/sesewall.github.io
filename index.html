Broccoli Cheese Soup

 

Chop 1 medium-large onion into very small pieces, and 4 carrots (peeled) into very thin rounds.

 

In a soup pot, lightly saute the onion and carrots in butter. Add 2 cloves minced garlic and cook about 30 seconds more. Remove from pot and set aside.

 

In the pot, melt a stick of butter, add 8 T flour, and cook over medium heat for about 3 to 5 minutes, whisking constantly, until flour is thickened.

 

Add a 32 oz box (4 cups) of chicken broth, whisking constantly. Thicken it a little.

 

Add 2 cups Half and Half and 2 cups milk, whisking constantly.

 

When it's hot, add a small bag of frozen broccoli florets (about 4 cups).

 

Add the other veggies back in.

Add 1.5 t salt, 1 t. pepper, 1 t. paprika, and a good sized pinch of cayenne.

 

Simmer 20-30 minutes. When broccoli gets soft, use a potato masher to mush it up and blend the flavors better. This makes it tastier.

 

Stir in an 8 oz bag of grated cheddar.

 

 

 

 

Marcella Hazan's Tomato Sauce

 

THE NEW YORK TIMES

YIELD 4 servings

TIME 1 hour

This is perhaps the most famous recipe created by Marcella Hazan, the cookbook author who changed how Americans cook Italian food. It also may be her easiest. Use your favorite canned tomatoes for this and don't be scared off by the butter. It gives the sauce an unparalleled velvety richness.

 

INGREDIENTS

 

2 cups tomatoes, in addition to their juices (for example, a 28-ounce can of San Marzano whole peeled tomatoes)

 

5 tablespoons butter

 

1 onion, peeled and cut in half

 

Salt

 

 

PREPARATION

 

Combine the tomatoes, their juices, the butter and the onion halves in a saucepan. Add a pinch or two of salt.

 

Place over medium heat and bring to a simmer. Cook, uncovered, for about 45 minutes. Stir occasionally, mashing any large pieces of tomato with a spoon. Add salt as needed.

 

If you wish, you can discard the onion before tossing the sauce with pasta.

Homemade Stuffing

 

INGREDIENTS:

 

10 slices sourdough bread, diced into 1/2-inch cubes

 

1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted

 

3 cloves garlic, minced

 

1 onion, diced

 

2 carrots, peeled and diced

 

2 stalks celery, diced

 

1/2 teaspoon dried sage

 

1/2 teaspoon dried thyme

 

1/4 teaspoon dried rosemary

 

Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste

 

1 cup chicken stock, or more, to taste

 

2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley leaves

 

 

DIRECTIONS:

 

Preheat oven to 400 degrees F. Spread bread cubes in a single layer on a baking sheet. Place into oven and bake until crisp and golden, about 4-5 minutes; set aside.

 

Melt butter in a large skillet over medium high heat. Add garlic, onion, carrots and celery. Cook, stirring occasionally, until tender. Stir in sage, thyme and rosemary until fragrant, about 1 minute.

 

Stir in bread cubes and gently toss to combine. Stir in chicken stock and parsley; cover and remove from heat.

 

Let sit for 10 minutes. Serve immediately.

 

 

 

 

Broiled Asian Chicken Thighs / Mediterranean Chicken Thighs

 

USE A FOIL COVERED BAKING SHEET FOR THIS - The SUGAR leaves a MESS on the broiler pan - almost impossible to clean.USE FOIL COVERED BAKING SHEET ONLY!

 

Marinate the chicken in savory, earthy Hoisin sauce, lime juice, a touch of sweet brown sugar, some rice vinegar, garlic, ginger, sesame oil, and a touch of spice from my beloved Sriracha hot sauce. (You can leave that out if you don't like spice.) It's still de-li-cious. Once that has a chance to do it's thing overnight, you simply pop these flavor bombs under the broiler for just 10-12 little minutes and dinner is served. Easy. Delicious. Quick.

 

Can I Cook Chicken Breasts in the Broiler?

If you prefer chicken breasts to thighs or chicken breasts are just what you have on hand, you're probably wondering if they will work in the broiler as well. The answer is, yes, they do. But, you're going to need a little more cooking time as the breasts are thicker and you'll have to remember to turn them so both sides get broiled. Rebrush with more sauce if you are worried that they'll dry out.

 

I wouldn't recommend broiling any thinner cuts of chicken, like tenders, simply because you'll end up with something resembling chicken jerky. Unless that's what you're going for. Now that I think about it, Asian chicken jerky sounds quite tasty.

 

How do you make Asian sauce sticky?

I like a good chicken thigh. The secret to these broiled chicken thighs though is making the sauce nice and sticky, so it stays on the chicken, rather than bakes off in a pool of sauce under the meat.

 

The brown sugar is key here. It will caramelize nicely with the rest of the ingredients (including the citrus from the lime juice) and that, plus the high heat will allow the sauce to stick nicely to your chicken. You could also use honey instead if you prefer.

 

Broiled Chicken thighs in Asian sauce on a sheet pan with foil.

 

Broiled Asian Chicken Thighs

 

1.33 lbs. boneless skinless chicken thighs, trimmed

 

3 tbsp. Hoisin sauce

 

2 tbsp. lime juice

 

1 tbsp. rice vinegar

 

1 tsp. brown sugar

 

1 garlic clove, minced

 

1 shallot, minced

 

1 tbsp. ginger, minced

 

2 tsp. sesame oil

 

1 tsp. Sriracha (optional)

 

 

For Mediterranean Chicken Thighs, use as the marinade:

 

1/2 c olive oil

 

Juice of two lemons

 

2 T red wine vinegar

 

4 cloves garlic (minced)

 

2 t oregano, plus salt and pepper.

 

 

Instructions

 

Mix together the Hoisin, lime juice, brown sugar, rice vinegar, garlic, shallot, ginger, and sesame oil. Marinate the chicken in this mixture for at least one hour, ideally overnight.

 

Preheat the oven on broil.

 

Remove the chicken from the marinade and let excess marinade drip off. Place on a foil covered baking sheet.

 

Broil 4-6 inches from the broiler for 5 minutes each side or until cooked through.

 

 

 

One Pot Lasagna Soup

 

INGREDIENTS

1 pound lean ground beef or half Italian sausage

 

1 yellow onion, diced

 

4-5 garlic cloves, minced

 

1/4 - 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes

 

1 24 oz. jar Prego

 

8-10 cups low sodium chicken broth, divided

 

1 14 oz. can crushed tomatoes

 

2 tablespoons tomato paste

 

2 teaspoons balsamic vinegar

 

1 1/2 teaspoons granulated sugar

 

1 tablespoon dried basil

 

1 tsp EACH dried parsley, dried oregano, salt

 

1/2 teaspoon pepper

 

1 whole bay leaf

 

10 uncooked lasagna noodles broken into approx. 1-2 inch pieces*

 

1/2 cup heavy cream

 

Cheese Garnish

 

Shredded mozzarella cheese

 

Freshly finely grated Parmesan cheese

 

Ricotta cheese

 

 

INSTRUCTIONS

 

Heat large Dutch oven/large soup pot over medium high heat. Add beef and onion and cook, stirring occasionally until beef is browned. Add garlic and red pepper flakes and saute for 30 seconds. Drain off any excess fat.

 

Add Prego Sauce, 6 cups chicken broth, crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, balsamic vinegar, sugar, spices and lasagna noodles. Bring to a boil then reduce to a simmer until lasagna noodles are tender, stirring occasionally (approximately 20-30 minutes).

 

Discard bay leaf and stir in heavy cream and 2-4 cups chicken broth to reach desired consistency. Garnish individual servings with desired amount of cheeses.

Bake 1.5 hours. Remove foil to brown a little in the last few minutes of cooking.

Country Style Steak

 

2 T vegetable oil

 

1 lb cube steak, cut into bite sized pieces

 

All purpose flour

 

1 envelope dry onion soup mix

 

2 cups water, or as needed

 

Salt, pepper, and garlic salt to taste

 

Heat the oil in a large skillet over medium high heat.Coat cube steak pieces with flour, and shake off the excess.

 

Fry steak pieces in the hot oil just until browned on each side. Set them aside.

 

Put a small amount of oil in the pan. Stir together the onion soup mix and 1 T of flour; sprinkle in with the oil and stir and cook till it starts to brown.

 

Gradually mix in the water to make a gravy. Return the cube steak to the pan and season with salt, pepper, and garlic salt.

 

Cover and simmer over low heat for about an hour.

3 cloves garlic, 2 T cumin, pinch cayenne

 

2 red peppers, chopped

 

2 c. brown rice

 

2 cans black beans, drained and rinsed

 

(not in original recipe, but can try adding two sliced zucchini)

Puerto Rican Chicken

 

2.25 lbs boneless, skinless chicken thighs, trimmed and cut into 2 inch pieces

 

4 Anaheim or poblano peppers, chopped

 

1 small onion, chopped

 

1 can tomato sauce

 

1 tomato, chopped

 

1 jar pimiento stuffed green olives, sliced

 

Fresh cilantro

 

8 c. water

 

2.5 c. brown rice

 

1 T olive oil

 

1 T oregano

 

1 t paprika

 

1 t salt

 

Cook the chicken, peppers, 1 small onion, 1 T oregano, 1 t paprika and 1 t salt in pan, about 3-5 min.

 

Add tomato sauce, tomato, olives and water and bring to a boil.

 

Stir in 2.5 c. rice and return to a boil.Simmer uncovered until the sauce is thick, the rice is tender and the chicken is cooked through - approximately 40-45 minutes.

 

Stir in cilantro and serve.

1 onion, chopped (optional)

 

salt and pepper to taste

 

4.5 tablespoons butter

Ham cubes, if making as a main dish

Korean Beef Bowl

 

⅛ c. brown sugar

 

1/4 c. soy sauce

 

2 t sesame oil

1 bunch green onions

 

Pinch of red pepper flakes

 

1/4 t. ground ginger

 

3 cloves garlic

Cook white rice with salt and sesame oil

Country Style Steak

 

2 T vegetable oil

 

1 lb cube steak, cut into bite sized pieces

 

All purpose flour

 

1 envelope dry onion soup mix

 

2 cups water, or as needed

 

Salt, pepper, and garlic salt to taste

 

Heat the oil in a large skillet over medium high heat. Coat cube steak pieces with flour, and shake off the excess.

 

Fry steak pieces in the hot oil just until browned on each side. Set them aside.

 

Put a small amount of oil in the pan. Stir together the onion soup mix and 1 T of flour; sprinkle in with the oil and stir and cook till it starts to brown.

 

Gradually mix in the water to make a gravy. Return the cube steak to the pan and season with salt, pepper, and garlic salt.

 

Cover and simmer over low heat for about an hour.

Chili

 

1 lb ground beef

 

1 medium onion

 

1 green pepper

 

2 regular size cans diced tomatoes

 

1 can black beans

 

1 can chicken broth

 

Frozen corn (optional)

 

Chili powder, cumin, garlic cloves

 

 

Brown 1 lb ground beef. When nearly done add 1 t chili powder and 1 t cumin. Remove to a plate.

 

1 medium onion cut small - put in pot with 1 T melted butter and saute, then add 1 cut up green pepper. When almost done, add 2 cloves garlic. Then add 1.5 t chili powder and stir.

 

Add 2 regular size cans diced tomatoes and 1 can black beans. Add 1 can chicken broth. Add 1 t chili powder and 1/2 t cumin. Boil. Then cook at a simmer for 10 min. Add frozen corn if using and return to a simmer.

 

Add the beef back in and cook 10-15 minutes more.

Brazilian Black Beans and Sausage

 

1 - 1.5 T oil

 

16 oz. Kielbasa, cut in small pieces

 

1 very large onion, chopped

 

3 cloves garlic, 2 T cumin, pinch cayenne

 

2 red peppers, chopped

 

2 c. brown rice

 

2 cans black beans, drained and rinsed

 

(not in original recipe, but can try adding two sliced zucchini)

 

Cook brown rice for 20 minutes in a separate pot.

 

Heat the oil in big pan and saute the onion a while, then add kielbasa and saute more.

 

Add all other ingredients, including pouring the rice and remaining water from the other pot into this one.

 

Bring to a boil, reduce heat to low, cover and simmer for 20-25 minutes.

 

If using white rice, skip the step of pre-cooking the rice part way separately, and just put the white rice in with everything else to start with.

 

Roasted cherry tomatoes with basil are a good side with this. Or sweet potato fries.

 

 

 

 

Puerto Rican Chicken

 

2.25 lbs boneless, skinless chicken thighs, trimmed and cut into 2 inch pieces

 

4 Anaheim or poblano peppers, chopped

 

1 small onion, chopped

 

1 can tomato sauce

 

1 tomato, chopped

 

1 jar pimiento stuffed green olives, sliced

 

Fresh cilantro

 

8 c. water

 

2.5 c. brown rice

 

1 T olive oil

 

1 T oregano

 

1 t paprika

 

1 t salt

 

Cook the chicken, peppers, 1 small onion, 1 T oregano, 1 t paprika and 1 t salt in pan, about 3-5 min.

 

Add tomato sauce, tomato, olives and water and bring to a boil.

 

Stir in 2.5 c. rice and return to a boil. Simmer uncovered until the sauce is thick, the rice is tender and the chicken is cooked through - approximately 40-45 minutes.

 

Stir in cilantro and serve.

 

 

 

 

Chicken Cacciatore

 

3 - 3.5 lbs chicken pieces

 

1 sliced medium onion

 

1 sliced medium green pepper

 

1 c. sliced mushrooms

 

1 clove garlic

 

28 oz can crushed tomatoes (or box Pomi Strained tomatoes)

 

Olive oil

 

1 t basil

 

1 t oregano

 

1/2 t salt and pepper to taste

 

 

Brown chicken in oil in large pan and remove it to a plate.

 

In oil remaining in skillet onion, green pepper, garlic and mushrooms. Return chicken to skillet.

 

Add crushed tomatoes and 1 t basil, 1 t oregano, 1/2 t salt and some pepper.

 

Heat to boiling then reduce heat. Simmer covered for 1 hour or until chicken is tender.

 

Thicken sauce if desired before serving. But probably just put a lot of parmesan with it!
